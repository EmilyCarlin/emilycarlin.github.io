Hello!

I'm Emily Carlin. I hail from the great state of Colorado, and now I live in Cambridge, MA.

I'm interested in designing experiences that empower people to create and express themselves. Right now, I work as a UX designer at Slalom in Boston. Before that, I was a UX designer at Tamr, a big data/machine learning startup in Cambridge.

And before that, I worked as a lead researcher for Dr. Sherry Turkle at MIT. I worked on her critically acclaimed book Reclaiming Conversation: The Power of Talk in a Digital Age. I also worked in the Tufts Human Computer Interaction Lab, where I focused on the practical use of brain-computer interfaces. I published a paper called Designing Brain-Computer Interfaces for Attention-Aware Systems in IEEE Computer.

I write about design on Medium and I teach user experience design classes at The Startup Institute in Boston. In fall of 2017 I was honored with the "student's choice" instructor award. I also mentor folks through Lesbians Who Tech and The Tech Jobs Tour. I'm passionate about making tech more inclusive.

I love to read, write, play the guitar, walk around town, and ride my bike.

I'm reading...

The Best Interface is No Interface by Golden Krishna. It's kind of a manifesto against "screen-based thinking." I find it inspiring, helpful, and fun to read. A lot of the time good design has nothing to do with a screen (or as Krishna calls them, "lazy rectangles.") It has changed the way I approach new projects, and reminded me that there's a lot more to consider when you think about experience design than "slapping an interface on it."

I'm learning...

Swift with Swift Playgrounds. Although this was technically designed for young people to learn to code, I find it SO fun and immersive.

I'm also honing my HTML/CSS/Javascript skills with the awesome SuperHi program. I built this very website with SuperHi skills!
