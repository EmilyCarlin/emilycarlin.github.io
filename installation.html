Streamlining the Appcues installation experience

Case Study

A bit of background

Appcues is a tool that allows people to build onboarding, feature announcements, surveys, and other experiences on top of their product without writing any code.

In order to publish the content they build with Appcues to their end uers, our customers need to install Appcues. Installing is a prerequisite for making the most of Appcues, but our instructions were causing a lot of confusion and unhappiness for our customers, not to mention our own customer success and support teams.

The story of building a better experience

I worked with a wonderful team of four engineers on a project to overhaul the Appcues installation experience. I led the effort from research to design to release.

The result of our efforts is live, but does require a (free!) Appcues account to access.

Understand the problem

I began by talking to people. How did the internal teams who work with customers every day think about the problem? In sessions with customer success, support, sales, and marketing folks, I began to uncover a map of the journey towards installation.

I also surveyed internal systems. I searched our support software and product feedback trackers for anything related to "installation." I also watched FullStory sessions of folks using the existing installation tool.

Refine the understanding

Journey maps are a great way to build shared understanding and make sure folks are aligned on what exactly the problem is that we are solving for.
